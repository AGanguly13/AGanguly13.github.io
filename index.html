The Beginning

May 12, 2020

My first experience with programming came when I took a half-year course on VisualBasic in my freshman year of highschool. At first the class was tough, and I struggled immensely. I just couldn't seem to wrap my head around programming. I remember that I would need to ask my classmates how to do even the simplest of tasks on most of our projects. However, the day before the final of the class, I finally decided to go to my father, a computer scientist, for help. Surprisingly, all it took was about an hour of explanation to get a better understanding of everything I had been taught in class. For the first time I did not feel overwhelmed about programming. I went in to class the next day full of confidence, and recieved an A on my final. I had actually began to enjoy coding but the problem was that I didn't know where to go from there.

Quarantine

June 3, 2020

After 2 years of not paying much mind to programming, a horrible and deadly pandemic somehow brought it back to me. Scrolling through Youtube one day a tutorial on Python caught my eye. As I was stuck in quarantine with nothing to do I decided: Why Not? . Not more than 15 minutes into the course, I remembered how I felt at the end of my VisualBasic class. I truly enjoyed programming. I finished the tutorial within a month, and had gained the fundamentals of Python.
